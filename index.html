What is alternative or complementary medicine?

Many patients choose to use treatments outside of conventional medicine. These may include traditional or herbal medicine developed from a variety of cultures such as traditional European or Chinese medicine.

The terms "alternative" and "complementary" medicine are often used interchangably. However, there is a distinction — alternative medicine refers to treatments used in place of conventional medicine while complementary medicine encompasses treatments used alongside conventional practices. The same practices can be considered "alternative" or "complementary" based on whether or not they are used with conventional medical treatment.

In this blog, we will be looking into various forms of alternative and complementary practices from a scientific perspective and deciding — are these treatments effective and safe alternatives to conventional medicine? In particular, we will be looking into the practice of naturopathy.

What is naturopathy?

Naturopathy originates from traditional European medical practices from the xxth century. It focuses on the use of natural remedies to leverage the body's natural healing process, emphasizing EEE. Naturopathic doctors are not medical doctors but do undergo 4 years of professional training in naturopathy. Naturopathy may involve: Using herbal medications Dietary and lifestyle changes Homeopathy - a form of alternative medicine Manipulative therapies - which are physical therapies Detoxification - through the use of foods and herbs to rid the body of toxins Psychotherapy Main principles of naturopathy Aiding in the body’s natural healing process by removing obstacles to healing Underlying symptoms: Rather than stopping symptoms, a naturopathic doctor can resolve underlying physical or mental issues by treating the body, mind, and spirit Treatments should be limited in adverse effects Treatment is on a holistic level - a doctor must focus on the overall health of an individual Lifestyle changes are an important instrument to preventing diseases
